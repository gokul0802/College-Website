MAHASHIVRATRI 2023

ॐ नमः शिवाय

Mahashivratri is one of the most important and widely celebrated Hindu festivals that is observed annually in honor of Lord Shiva. It falls on the 14th night of the new moon in the Hindu month of Phalguna or Maagh, which usually falls between February and March in the Gregorian calendar. The festival has great religious and cultural significance in Hinduism and is celebrated with great enthusiasm and devotion by millions of Hindus across the world. On Mahashivratri, devotees of Lord Shiva fast, perform puja, and observe vigil all night long. The festival is celebrated to seek the blessings of Lord Shiva, who is considered to be the destroyer of evil and the transformer of the universe. Lord Shiva is one of the three primary deities in the Hindu pantheon, along with Brahma and Vishnu, and is revered as the supreme lord of all creation. During the festival, devotees worship Lord Shiva by offering milk, honey, flowers, and other offerings. They also chant mantras and perform a special aarti in honor of Lord Shiva. The festival is believed to be a time when Lord Shiva is most easily pleased and is said to grant the wishes of his devotees. Mahashivratri also has many mythological and cultural significance in Hinduism. It is believed that on this day, Lord Shiva performed the Tandava, the cosmic dance of destruction, and got married to Goddess Parvati. The festival is also associated with the renewal of the cosmic energy and the awakening of spiritual consciousness. Overall, Mahashivratri is a day of great religious significance and is celebrated with great fervor and devotion by Hindus across the world.

SHIVJAYANTI 2023

|| जय भवानी जय शिवाजी ||

Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Jayanti is an annual celebration observed in the state of Maharashtra, India to commemorate the birth anniversary of the legendary warrior king, Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It is celebrated on February 19th every year with great enthusiasm and pride. On this day, people across Maharashtra pay tribute to the great king by offering prayers and flowers at his statues and monuments. The day begins with the hoisting of the saffron flag, a symbol of Maratha sovereignty, in public places, followed by the recitation of Shivaji Maharaj's hymns and the playing of traditional instruments. Several cultural and social events are also organized to celebrate the occasion, including processions, folk dances, and plays showcasing the life and achievements of Shivaji Maharaj. People dress up in traditional Maratha attire, and feasts are organized in many households, where people exchange greetings and sweets. The day is not only celebrated in Maharashtra but also in other parts of India, where Marathi-speaking communities exist. It is a reminder of Shivaji Maharaj's legacy, which inspired generations of Indians to fight for their rights and resist foreign rule. It is a day to honor his bravery, patriotism, and leadership and to celebrate the rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra.
